Introduction

Mini Go is the game of Go played on the 9 x 9 board under Chinese rules (komi being 7). Ordinary people can become a Mini Go player within a few minutes, due to the few simple rules of Go. However, to achieve the lowest master level (1 Dan), it will probably take several years for playing some 5,000 - 8,000 games of Mini Go.

This is due in large part to the 9 x 9 Go board that make Mini Go comparable to chess in complexity—the number of paths down the move tree has been estimated to be at 10120 in chess, and the game rules that scarely describe how Mini Go is actually played, as noted by Zobrist (1969) . Read more

5 Tips to Get Better at Mini Go

Photography Tips

First, keep in mind that knowing only the rules of Go is not enough to get better at Mini Go. The rules do not describe how Go is best played. They just describe the objects of the game, how Go stones are placed and captured, how a perfectly safe group are created, how the game ends, how White's disadvantange of playing second is compensated, and how the final scores are determined. They scarely describe strategy and tactics of the game.

Second, you need to gain some basic knoweldge of tactics and strategy, by playing five thousand games of 9 x 9 Go online, where many stronger players are ready to beat you. You would better understand Mini Go by learning from your own bad moves that usually make you fail and your good moves that usually make you win. Note that a majority of your moves are not the best moves, and your kyu-like thinking will be a major obstacle that prevents you from breakthrough to Shodan. You will need to unlearn and relearn for the better way to Mini Go.

Third, consult a real Go master, a verified Go bot that has already achieved superhuman performance and topped the chart of a standard online Go server (verified Go AI). The verified Go AI should be comparable to KataTest, 9 Dan in superhuman performance—capable of becoming No. 1 top ranked player on Go Quest (92 win, 0 lost, and 1 draws).

Fourth, have your games analyzed by the versified Go AI, for best move detection and game situation analysis. Make understanding of why the best moves are there, and formulate the principles of 9 x 9 Go to describe the best moves, which can be applied to your games in the future.

Fifth, create the proverbs to describe the principles of 9 x 9 Go, to remind yourself of how to play Go better. The proverbs should come with Go diagrams and detailed descriptions, or you will forget what your proverbs really mean.
