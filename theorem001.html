9 × 9 Go Theorems

Theorem 1 Neural Network Performance Check

Let a proper komi for 9 × 9 Go is 7. If komi is 7.5 and the neural network has been trained to perfection, White's win rate will be 100% at Move 1. And, the weaker neural network, the lower win rate for White at Move 1.

Proof

We know that the newer approved neural networks are stronger than the old ones. Hence, we have the neural networks arranged by strength: 170e_b10s114 < 170e_b20s530 < Kata1_b18nbts550. The win rates for White at Move 1 of these neural networks are a, b, and c, respectively, where a < b < c. Superhuman A Master of Go 9p+ showed that a = 62%; b = 85%; c = 88%.

Insight

The performace of the new naural network, Kata1_b18nbts550, as of March 30, 2023, is 88% of the maximum achievable performance. This theorem can be useful for checking how well a neural network has been trained, by showing the performance gap between the theoretically perfect neural network and the one being tested.
